Dr. Oktay BAHADIR

Human Right Lawyer

Oktay Bahadır is an independent human rights expert providing litigation consultancy services for the applicants seeking redress before the Turkish Constitutional Court and the European Court of Human Rights, as well as various UN treaty bodies. He has a proven academic and practical expertise in international human rights law as well as criminal law.

Apart from his independent career, he has over 10 years’ professional experience covering the Turkish judicial service and justice administration, as well as his international assignment for a two-month period at the European Court of Human Rights.

From 2006 to 2016, he worked as a prosecutor in various provinces and districts. For more than three-year-period, between 2011 and 2014, he was employed at the Turkish Ministry of Justice as a rapporteur judge dealing mainly with preparation of Government observations submitted to the Turkish Constitutional Court and the European Court of Human Rights. Along with his professional works at the Ministry of Justice, he lectured classes of human rights at the Turkish Justice Academy for nearly two years.

He holds a master’s degree on public law obtained from Gazi University in Ankara. He received his doctorate on human rights law in 2014. He studied English in the United States for one year. He has published numerous articles in various periodicals and on the Internet and published a book named “The Right to Life”. He shares his views and assessments on topical human rights matters on his personal blog and Twitter account.

Oktay Bahadır, who is married and has three children, is fluent in English and French.

    Examining the Ombudsman Institution with Sweden, England and France,
    Journal of the Turkish Justice Academy, April 2010

          Ombudsman, is an institution that studies the complaints coming from the public, which is related to procedures and deeds that the administration undertakes, and searches for solution ways. Its function has been not to leave the person alone against the administration and to defend his/ her rights. Ombudsman is equipped with the authorities such as investigating and researching, criticizing, announcing the problematic sides of administration to the parliament and public opinion. This article contains Ombudsman corporation as an administrative control tool, its historical background, some examples of this corporation in the World and discussions about its applicability in Turkey.


    Offence of Exceeding The Limits of Authorisation For Use of Force,
    Journal of the Turkish Justice Academy, October 2012

          In this article, particularly notion of use of force and its conditions are examined. Then, elements of the offence of “Exceeding the Limits of Authorisation for Use of Force” regulated under article 256 of the Turkish Criminal Code are discussed together with different views in the doctrine and judgments of the Court of Cassation. Furthermore, when appropriate, case law of the European Court of Human Rights with regard to the matter is also mentioned.

    Authority for the Use of Force and Its Limitations within the Scope of Article 2 of the European Convention on Human Rights,
    Human Rights Review, December 2012

          The main objective of this article is to reflect the viewpoint of the Court in regard to the use of force which could be considered as being in breach of the right to life. For that reason, the law-enforcement officers’ authority to use force and its limitations will be assessed pursuant to Article 2 of the Convention. Moreover, the judgments especially relating to Article 3 will be referred when appropriate. Apart from the judgments considered as well-established case-law, recent judgments rendered by the Court will also be referred in this Article as far as possible. Firstly, the terms of “absolute necessity” and “strict proportionality” will be explained in the light of the Court’s case-law. Subsequently, the exceptions for the right to life set out in paragraph 2 of Article 2 will be handled. Meanwhile, the issue on planning of the operations will be discussed under a different heading. Lastly, injuring carrying a potential risk of death, protection of right to life by law, training of the security forces and burden of proof will be discussed.


    Authorısatıon for Use of Prıvate Securıty Forces
    Uyuşmazlık Mahkemesi Dergisi, June 2014

          The authorities of the private security forces and the limitations of these authorities are primarily discussed in this article. The authority for use of force of the private security forces is subsequently examined. Moreover, the offences committed both against and by the private security officers while exercising their authority for use of force are addressed in this article. When appropriate, the judgments rendered by the Court of Cassation in this regard are also referred.
